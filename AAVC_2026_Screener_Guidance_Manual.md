## Part 1: How to Log In & Navigate the Portal

### 1. Portal Access & Initial Login
1. Open the **AAVC Screening Portal** URL in your web browser (Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, or Safari recommended).
2. On the **Alumni & Screener Login** screen, enter your credentials:
   - **Email Address**: Enter your registered email address exactly (e.g., `adelaide.masu@uct.ac.za`).
   - **Password**: Your initial login password has been pre-configured to be your **exact email address in all lowercase letters** (e.g., `adelaide.masu@uct.ac.za`).
   - *(Note: The portal features smart case-sensitivity handling, so typing your email or password with capital letters will automatically log you in cleanly).*
3. Click **Log In**. You will be taken immediately to your personalized **Screening Workspace**.

### 2. Understanding Your Screening Workspace
Once logged in, the portal automatically filters applications to display only the countries and provinces assigned specifically to you:
- **Cohort / Country Tabs**: At the top of your workspace, you will see buttons for your assigned cohorts (e.g., `Nigeria`, `SA-WC`, `Kenya`). Click any tab to switch between your assigned pools.
- **Applicant Leaderboard**: Below the tabs, applicants for the selected country are listed. The table displays their name, institution, current position, total score, decision status (`Accept`, `Pending`, `Reject`), and their country rank.
- **Sorting & Prioritization**: As you score and rank candidates, the portal automatically sorts the table by **Score & Decision Priority**, giving you a real-time leaderboard of the top candidates in each country.

## Part 2: Step-by-Step Screening & Scoring Workflow

To begin evaluating an applicant, click the blue **`✏️ Review / Score`** button next to their name. This opens their interactive **Evaluation & Scoring Card**.

### Step 1 – Check Prior Attendance & Demographic Profile
Before evaluating their technical qualifications, verify whether the candidate has attended AAVC in person previously:
1. **Automated Alumni Match**: Look at the header bar of the candidate's evaluation card. The system automatically cross-references the candidate's name and email against our 5-year historical AAVC alumni database (`Alumni Match` badge).
2. **Self-Reported Attendance**: Review what the candidate checked under **Previous AAVC** (`Applicant self-reported attending AAVC previously`).
3. **Outright Rejection Rule**: If it is confirmed that the applicant has attended the AAVC in person before, select **`Yes (-1 pt - DQ)`** under *Attended AAVC previously?*, set the **Decision** to **`Reject`**, and enter a comment stating `"Previously attended course"`. You do not need to score the remaining sections. If they have **not** attended in person, proceed to Step 2.

---

### Step 2 – Open & Verify Application Documents
Under the candidate's profile section, you will find direct **Clickable Document Links** (hosted securely via JotForm). Click each link to view the submitted files in a new browser tab:
- **📄 Application Form**: Review their self-reported demographic details, line manager information, and motivation.
- **📑 Curriculum Vitae (CV)**: Must be **strictly maximum 2 pages**.
- **✉️ Motivation Letter**: Must be **strictly maximum 1 page**.
- **📋 Recommendation Letter**: Must be **strictly maximum 1 page**.

---

### Step 3 – Score the Rubric Criteria
Using the interactive scoring card, assign points for each criterion based on the official AAVC scoring guidelines. The portal automatically calculates the total score (Maximum possible: **6 points**).

| Rubric Criterion | Point Options & Selection Guidance |
| :--- | :--- |
| **1. Attended AAVC Previously?** | • **No (+1 pt)**: Candidate has not attended AAVC before.<br>• **Yes (-1 pt - Automatic Rejection)**: Candidate previously attended in person. |
| **2. Current Position & Involvement** | • **2 pts**: Works **directly** in human vaccine research or immunization systems.<br>• **1 pt**: Works **indirectly** in human vaccine research (e.g., general health-related or public health field).<br>• **0 pts**: Non-human vaccine work (e.g., veterinary vaccines) or non-vaccine work. |
| **3. Curriculum Vitae (CV)** | • **1 pt**: CV is **strictly maximum 2 pages** AND clearly demonstrates current involvement or a clear career pathway toward involvement in vaccines/immunization systems.<br>• **0 pts**: Exceeds 2 pages OR lacks relevance to vaccines/immunization. |
| **4. Motivation Letter** | • **1 pt**: Letter is **strictly maximum 1 page** AND clearly demonstrates strong need for training (*capacity to benefit*) as well as the ability to contribute during and after AAVC (*e.g., transmitting learnings to colleagues/institution*).<br>• **0 pts**: Exceeds 1 page OR lacks compelling motivation/justification. |
| **5. Recommendation Letter** | • **1 pt**: Letter is **strictly maximum 1 page**, ideally from their direct line manager/supervisor, written on **official signed letterhead**, and strongly recommends the candidate with concrete evidence.<br>• **0 pts**: Exceeds 1 page, unofficial/unsigned, or weak support. |

### Step 4 – Select Standardized Comments & Add Notes
Instead of typing comments from scratch for every applicant, the portal provides a **Multi-Select Comments Bank** grouped by decision tier. Click one or more standardized badges to attach them to the evaluation, or type specific notes in the text area below:

- **🟢 Acceptance / High Priority Presets**:
  - `Directly involved in national immunization program`
  - `Strong application – high potential & clear pathway`
  - `High capacity to benefit & transmit learnings`
  - `Excellent institutional recommendation`
- **🟡 Review / Reserve Pool Presets**:
  - `Good qualifications but needs more practical vaccine exposure`
  - `Strong clinical background; indirect vaccine involvement`
  - `Meets criteria; placed on reserve list`
- **🔴 Rejection / Disqualification Presets**:
  - `Weak application – lacks relevant experience`
  - `Non-human/veterinary vaccine focus`
  - `Incomplete documents or exceeds strict page limits`
  - `Previously attended course`

---

### Step 5 – Set Decision Status & Country Rank
Before saving, make your recommendation and assign their numerical rank relative to other applicants from the same country:

1. **Assign Decision**: Select from `Accept`, `Pending / Reserve`, or `Reject`.
2. **Assign Rank (`Rank in Country`)**: Enter their numerical ranking (`1` for top-ranked candidate, `2` for second, `3`, etc.).
   - *CRITICAL*: **You must rank ALL applicants from the same country sequentially**, including rejected or reserve candidates! This numerical ranking allows the committee to immediately extend invitations to the next-highest ranked candidate (`#3`, `#4`, etc.) if a top-ranked applicant (`#1` or `#2`) is unable to attend.
3. Click **💾 Save Score & Evaluation**. Your scores, comments, decision, and rank are saved instantly to the cloud database and reflected on the live country leaderboard.

## Part 3: Official AAVC Selection & Ranking Rules

When finalizing your assigned countries, please strictly enforce the following selection thresholds:

1. **Rank Every Applicant**: Every applicant within a country must receive a sequential numerical rank (`1`, `2`, `3`, etc.) based on your total evaluation score and holistic review.
2. **Top Two Candidates Per Country Limit**:
   - **Rank #1 Candidate** → Recommend **`Accept`** (Prioritized for **In-Person Attendance**).
   - **Rank #2 Candidate** → Recommend **`Accept` (or `Pending/Reserve`)** (Prioritized for **Online Attendance**, or in-person if the candidate is exceptionally strong and funding permits).
   - **Rank #3 and lower** → Recommend **`Reject` (or `Pending/Reserve`)**. Ensure their numerical rank (`3`, `4`, `5`, etc.) is recorded so they can serve as alternates.
3. **Single-Applicant Countries**: If there is only one applicant from an assigned country, conduct the full screening process and recommend **`Accept`** (Rank `1`) if they meet the criteria, or **`Reject`** if they do not meet the minimum threshold.
